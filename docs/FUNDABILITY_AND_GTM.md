---

## The core problem to solve in the pitch

Central Asian / Uzbek funds are **not** Silicon Valley: revenue-sensitive, conservative, smaller checks, and they want a *who-pays-and-when* answer early. Read cold, *"free consumer scam-checker bot, donations maybe, B2B someday"* lands as **"cute consumer app, unclear business."** Three red flags they'll raise:

1. **No revenue thesis to underwrite** — B2B is out-of-scope and described as "someday."
2. **Weak retention segment** — by our own admission (one-off consumers).
3. **Solo founder + consumer freebie** — pattern-matches to a side project.

The product is fine. The **framing and proof points** decide the raise.

---

## The reframe

> **"Anti-fraud data infrastructure for Central Asia's digital-commerce boom — and the consumer app is our data-acquisition engine."**

Why it works *right now*: Uzum became the first unicorn; Click / Payme / Uzcard / Humo are scaling; e-commerce and BNPL are exploding — and **fraud scales with all of it.** Banks, marketplaces, and fintechs have real fraud losses and real budgets. The bot becomes the **unfair data advantage** (informal Telegram / Instagram / p2p fraud signal their internal teams can't collect), not the business itself.

---

## What to do — ranked by leverage

1. **Land 1–2 B2B design partners or LOIs.** A single signed "we would pay for this" letter from a marketplace / fintech / payment player is worth more than 10,000 bot users. Start now, in parallel with the build.
2. **Get into IT Park Uzbekistan** — tax benefits, credibility, grants, demo days, investor access. *(Verify current terms.)*
3. **Prove the moat compounds** — headline metric: **graph-match rate trending up week-over-week.**
4. **Turn regulation into credibility** — comply with the Personal Data Law (local storage), never store full card numbers, written retention/privacy policy. Pre-empts the bank's #1 objection.
5. **De-risk the solo founder** — a domain advisor (fintech / banking / anti-fraud) and a co-founder / first-hire plan.
6. **Regional expansion narrative** — UZ → Kazakhstan (bigger VC market, same scams) → broader CA/CIS. The *build* stays UZ-only; the *pitch* shows the path.
7. **Tie to the digitalization thesis** — "as commerce digitizes, fraud scales; we're the trust/risk layer underneath."

## Metrics that make it fundable

- **Consumer pull:** WAU/MAU, 2nd-check-in-14-days retention, organic growth, ~zero CAC.
- **Moat:** graph entities/edges, **graph-match rate trending up**, report volume, verified reports/week.
- **B2B signal:** # design partners / LOIs, one paid pilot, pipeline value.
- **Unit economics:** cost-per-check trending **down**.
- **Impact:** # users who **confirmed they avoided a payment**.

## B2B discovery — run in parallel with v1 (not "later")

Interview **~5 prospective buyers** — marketplaces, fintechs/banks, high-volume Telegram sellers. Learn what they'd actually pay for:
- real-time check API · batch checks · fraud digest · seller watchlist · investigation dashboard.

## What would kill the raise

- Pitching donations as revenue.
- Leading with "AI" instead of "data" (they've seen the wrappers).
- No design partner / LOI.
- No regulatory answer while handling cards and personal data.
- "Uzbekistan-only, no expansion path."
